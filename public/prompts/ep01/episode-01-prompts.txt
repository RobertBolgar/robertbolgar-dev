3. Layout and Alignment Improvements Prompt

[ACTUAL PROMPT]

Fix the alignment issues on the homepage.

The current design looks good overall, but some text areas and card grids are pushed too far to one side.

Goal:
Make all sections feel intentionally centered and balanced.

Do not redesign the site.
Do not change the content.
Do not add new sections.

Fix layout alignment for:
- Latest Videos section
- Projects I'm Building section
- Devlog section
- About Robert section
- Footer if needed

Requirements:
1. Create consistent section containers
- Each major section should use the same max-width container.
- Section headings and intro paragraphs should be centered.
- Card grids should align cleanly under the section header.
- Avoid text blocks sitting too far left unless intentionally designed.

2. Fix card grid alignment
- Videos and Projects cards should center within their section.
- On desktop, cards should use a balanced grid.
- On tablet/mobile, cards should stack cleanly.
- Do not leave a single card awkwardly stuck on the left if the row is incomplete.
- Center incomplete rows when there are only 3 cards in a 2-column layout.

3. Devlog alignment
- Keep the devlog readable.
- The heading and intro text should be centered.
- The devlog list can be left-aligned, but it should sit inside a centered max-width container.

4. About alignment
- Center the heading and body copy.
- Keep the text width narrow enough to read comfortably.

5. Footer alignment
- Make sure footer content is balanced.
- It should not feel pushed against either side.

CSS guidance:
- Use reusable classes like .section, .section-inner, .section-header, .section-copy, .card-grid.
- Prefer max-width, margin: 0 auto, and consistent padding.
- Avoid hard-coded left margins that break alignment.
- Keep the current dark premium style.

After implementation:
- Check desktop layout.
- Check tablet layout.
- Check mobile layout.
- Confirm headings, intro text, cards, devlog, and footer feel centered and balanced.
- Give me a brief summary of what changed.
